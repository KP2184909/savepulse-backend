# SavePulse Content Bible

Last reviewed: 2026-06-22

Status: Organic content and future advertising strategy for review before public launch. This document does not authorize production, email, pricing, scheduler, checkout, or advertising changes.

## 1. Core Positioning

SavePulse is a decision-support tool that helps people check exchange-rate, gold, and BTC context before making their own decisions. It summarizes observations from historical data in plain language.

SavePulse is not:

- A trading signal.
- Investment or financial advice.
- A money exchange service.
- A broker or execution platform.
- A prediction or guarantee of future rates.

Every piece of content should help a beginner answer:

1. What am I deciding?
2. What context should I check first?
3. Why can a small rate difference matter?
4. What can SavePulse help me understand without making the decision for me?

## 2. Big Idea

Primary Thai idea:

> เช็กก่อนแลก ลดโอกาสเสียเปรียบโดยไม่รู้ตัว

Supporting English idea:

> The hidden cost of timing

The emotional tension is not fear of missing a speculative return. It is the discomfort of making a large exchange decision without checking useful context first.

## 3. The Enemy

The enemy is not a bank, broker, exchange shop, payment provider, or any specific institution.

The enemy is:

> Making a large exchange decision without checking context first.

Content should not attack providers or claim that SavePulse offers the final executable rate. Actual rates, fees, and spreads vary by provider.

## 4. Content Pillars

### 4.1 รู้งี้ก่อนแลก

Short stories about information people wish they had checked before exchanging. Focus on learning and preparation, not blame.

### 4.2 เงินก้อนเดียวกัน มูลค่าไม่เท่ากัน

Show how a small difference in the reference rate can affect the estimated final amount. Label every scenario as an example and exclude provider fees unless explicitly included.

### 4.3 ก่อนเที่ยว ก่อนแลกเยน ดอลลาร์ หรือยูโร

Travel-led content about budgeting, timing flexibility, partial preparation, and checking context before converting travel money.

### 4.4 Freelancer And Foreign-Income Users

Explain how people receiving USD, EUR, or other foreign income can review the THB context before deciding when to convert their own funds.

### 4.5 Gold Buyers

Help users separate emotional reactions from historical context. Keep gold content educational and secondary during early public testing.

### 4.6 BTC Emotional Control

Focus on pausing, checking context, and avoiding emotion-led decisions. Do not present SavePulse as a crypto recommendation product.

### 4.7 SME Invoice Cost Impact

Explain how currency movement may change estimated THB invoice cost for import/export SMEs and finance teams. Show assumptions, due dates, rate source, and exclusions.

### 4.8 Morning Daily Pulse Ritual

Position Daily Pulse as a brief morning context check that reduces the need to monitor charts. Do not promise a fixed delivery time until scheduling is operationally verified.

## 5. Safe Wording

- ลดโอกาสเสียเปรียบ
- ข้อสังเกตจากข้อมูลย้อนหลัง

- เริ่มน่าจับตา

- ใช้ประกอบการตัดสินใจ
- ไม่รับประกันเรทในอนาคต

### Avoid

- กำไร
- ทำกำไร
- สัญญาณซื้อขาย
- สัญญาณ
- ซื้อเลย
- ขายเลย
- แลกเลย
- ต้องแลก
- จุดเข้า
- พลาดกำไร
- ได้เปรียบสุดๆ
- ไม่พลาดโอกาส
- รับประกัน, except inside a clear disclaimer such as `ไม่รับประกันเรทในอนาคต`
- profit
- guaranteed
- buy signal
- sell signal
- entry
- take profit

### Claim Rules

- Use real data or clearly label a number as an example.
- Show the observation window and timestamp when presenting current information.
- Do not invent user counts, scarcity, testimonials, savings, or confidence percentages.
- Do not imply that a reference rate is the rate a user will receive.
- Mention that actual provider rates, fees, and spreads may differ.
- Do not use urgency unless it comes from a real user deadline or invoice due date.
- Keep the disclaimer readable rather than hiding it in a caption or fast final frame.

## 6. Short-Form Clip Structure

### Default 15–25 Second Clip

- **0–2 seconds — Hook:** present a relatable question, amount, or overlooked detail.
- **3–7 seconds — Pain:** show why making the decision without context can feel uncomfortable.
- **8–13 seconds — Insight:** explain one useful historical or amount-based observation.
- **14–18 seconds — SavePulse demo:** show one clear card, conversion, or Daily Pulse view.
- **19–25 seconds — CTA:** invite the viewer to check before deciding.

Example safe CTA:

> ก่อนตัดสินใจเรื่องเงินก้อน ลองเช็กบริบทของเรทก่อนที่ SavePulse

### Additional Formats

- **6–8 seconds — Hook clip:** one question, one amount contrast, one simple CTA.
- **20–35 seconds — Demo clip:** show a real task from input to plain-language result.
- **30–45 seconds — Story clip:** person, deadline, amount, missed context, and lesson.
- **60–90 seconds — Founder explainer:** why SavePulse exists, what it does, what it does not do, and who it helps.

## 8. Organic-First Plan

Do not run paid ads yet.

### First 30 Days

- Publish one short clip per day.
- Reuse the same core edit on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts with platform-appropriate captions.
- Use the founder's personal Facebook profile for transparent Private Beta context and conversation.
- Test one major variable at a time: hook, use case, proof format, or CTA.
- Reply to genuine questions and turn repeated confusion into future clips.
- Record watch time, completion, profile or link clicks, signups, and feedback quality.
- Do not change production after every comment; batch evidence before proposing changes.

### Suggested 30-Day Mix

- 8 currency and amount-difference clips.
- 5 travel clips.
- 4 freelancer or foreign-income clips.
- 3 SME invoice clips.
- 2 gold clips.
- 2 BTC emotional-control clips.
- 3 Daily Pulse or product demos.
- 2 founder or build-in-public clips.
- 1 direct Private Beta invitation.

## 9. Paid Ads Later

Paid advertising may only be considered after:

- The household pilot passes.
- Ten real beta users sign up.
- A controlled manual Daily Pulse batch succeeds.
- Email logs and unsubscribe behavior are reviewed.
- At least 20–30 organic clips have been published and measured.
- Repeatable winning hooks and use cases are identified.
- The landing page converts without confusing users about the product.
- The owner explicitly approves a paid test.

## 10. Future Paid-Ad Guardrails

- Avoid copy that resembles trading or investment advice.
- Do not lead with crypto- or gold-heavy ads during the first paid tests.
- Start with travel currency and freelancer or foreign-income use cases.
- Use real product screens and clearly labeled examples.
- Do not target people through fear, financial distress, or unrealistic outcomes.
- Do not use fake testimonials, fake activity, fake scarcity, or unsupported savings.
- Review current ad-platform financial-services policies before launch.
- Send paid traffic only to an approved, stable landing experience.

Possible later test budget, subject to explicit approval:

- Thailand: ฿300–500 per day.
- International: $10–15 per day.

Treat this as a capped learning budget, not a commitment. Define stop conditions before spending.

## 11. Visual Direction

- Premium fintech rather than trading-terminal aesthetics.
- Dark teal with mint or aqua glow.
- Mobile email and decision-card UI as the visual product proof.
- Clean dopamine cues: reveal, completion, clarity, and a rewarding CTA.
- High contrast, readable type, and restrained motion.
- Trustworthy and calm, never scam-like or trading-guru styled.
- Avoid excessive neon, coins, rockets, flashing charts, exaggerated arrows, or alarm colors.

## 12. Metrics

Track per platform and per clip:

- **3-second hold:** viewers remaining after three seconds divided by starts.
- **Average watch time:** average seconds watched.
- **Completion rate:** completed views divided by starts.
- **Profile or link clicks:** number and rate from viewers.
- **Signups:** completed SavePulse registrations attributable to the content where measurable.
- **Email opens:** directional only; privacy protections may make open tracking incomplete.
- **Feedback quality:** count of responses that identify a real use case, confusion, or actionable need.

Also record:

- Clip topic and format.
- Hook text.
- CTA text.
- Video duration.
- Platform and publish time.
- Whether numbers were examples or current data.
- Comments showing misunderstanding or perceived financial-advice risk.

Do not optimize for views alone. The primary early signal is whether relevant people understand the product, sign up, and provide useful feedback.
