# SavePulse Agent Instructions

SavePulse is not a forex signal product, trading platform, investment advisor, or money exchange service.

SavePulse is a beginner-friendly exchange-rate decision assistant. The product helps users check whether today’s exchange rate looks favorable, neutral, or unfavorable before they exchange money.

## Product positioning

Use simple human language. Avoid technical trading language as the main user-facing copy.

Good framing:
- Check before you exchange
- Don’t exchange blindly
- Good time to exchange
- Wait for now
- Exchanging now may be less favorable
- Today’s rate looks relatively favorable
- Set an alert for a better rate
- Based on recent historical data

Avoid:
- Forex signal
- Buy signal
- Sell signal
- Entry point
- Profit guaranteed
- Make money from exchange rates
- Win rate
- Trading strategy
- Guaranteed return

## UI/UX principle

The main UI should answer the user’s question directly.

Instead of showing technical labels first, show plain-language decisions:

1. Good time to exchange
   Supporting copy: Today’s rate looks relatively favorable compared with recent historical data.

2. Wait for now
   Supporting copy: Exchanging now may be less favorable. If you are not in a hurry, consider waiting and setting an alert.

3. Not urgent yet
   Supporting copy: The rate is not clearly favorable or unfavorable. If you need to exchange soon, consider exchanging partially.

Technical pair names such as EUR/USD, USD/JPY, or XAU/USD may appear only as secondary/advanced information.

## Legal-safe copy

Every decision card should include or link to this disclaimer:

SavePulse provides decision-support information based on historical exchange-rate data. It is not financial advice, investment advice, trading instruction, or a guarantee of future exchange rates. Actual rates, fees, and spreads may vary by provider.

## UX goal

A beginner should understand the page within 5 seconds:

- What money they have
- What money they want
- Whether now looks good, neutral, or unfavorable
- What action to take next
- Why setting an alert is useful

Always prioritize clarity over financial complexity.
